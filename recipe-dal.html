Dal Tadka

The most beloved lentil dish from the Indian subcontinent — creamy yellow dal finished with a smoky ghee tempering of cumin, garlic, and dried chilies.

⏱ 35 min
🍽 Easy
👤 4 servings
🥦 Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • Dal:
  • 200g yellow split lentils (chana dal or toor dal)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp red chilli powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 750ml water
  • Tadka (Tempering):
  • 3 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Fresh coriander to garnish

Instructions

1

Wash & Soak the Lentils

Rinse the lentils in cold water 3–4 times until the water runs clear. Soak for 20 minutes. This reduces cooking time and makes them creamier.

2

Cook the Base

Heat 1 tbsp oil in a deep pot. Sauté the onion until golden brown. Add tomatoes and cook until mushy. Add turmeric, coriander powder, chilli powder, and salt. Cook for 2 minutes.

3

Simmer the Dal

Drain the lentils and add to the pot. Pour in water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and creamy. Add more water if needed.

4

Mash Slightly

Using the back of a ladle, mash some of the lentils against the pot wall. This creates the perfect creamy-yet-chunky texture. Taste and adjust salt.

5

The Tadka (The Star of the Dish)

In a small pan, heat ghee over high heat until shimmering. Add cumin seeds — they should splutter immediately. Add garlic slices and fry until golden. Add dried chilies and asafoetida. Swirl for 10 seconds.

6

Combine & Serve

Pour the sizzling tadka directly over the dal. It will hiss dramatically — that's the moment! Stir gently to mix. Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve hot with steamed rice or naan.
