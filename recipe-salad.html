Classic Greek Salad

Crisp cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, briny olives, and a thick slab of feta, dressed with nothing but excellent olive oil and dried oregano.

⏱ 15 min
🍽 Easy
👤 3 servings
🌿 No cooking

Ingredients

  • 3 large ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 large English cucumber, sliced thick
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into rings
  • ½ red onion, sliced thin
  • 100g Kalamata olives, whole or pitted
  • 150g block feta cheese
  • 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried Greek oregano
  • ½ tsp flaky sea salt
  • Fresh cracked black pepper
  • Optional: 1 tsp red wine vinegar

Instructions

1

Prep the Vegetables

Cut tomatoes into chunky wedges. Slice cucumber thickly on the diagonal — about 1cm. Slice bell pepper into rounds. Thinly slice the red onion. Do not chop things too small — Greek salad is rustic and chunky.

2

Arrange in a Wide Bowl

Spread tomatoes on the bottom of a wide serving bowl or platter. Layer cucumber, pepper rings, and red onion on top. Scatter olives throughout.

3

Add the Feta Block

The authentic Greek way: place the entire block of feta on top rather than crumbling it. This keeps it creamy and lets people break off pieces as they go.

4

Dress It

Drizzle olive oil generously over everything, including the feta. Sprinkle dried oregano on top. Add sea salt and cracked black pepper. A few drops of red wine vinegar is optional but lovely.

5

Serve Immediately

Serve right away with crusty bread to mop up the dressing at the bottom. Do not toss beforehand — the layered look is part of the charm. If resting, add dressing at the last minute.
