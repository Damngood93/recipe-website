Gajar Ka Halwa

⏱ 60 min 🍽 Medium 👤 6 servings 🍮 Dessert

Ingredients

  • 1kg carrots (preferably red Delhi carrots)
  • 1 litre full-fat milk
  • 150g sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 4 tbsp ghee
  • 6 cardamom pods, crushed
  • 2 tbsp khoya / mawa (optional but recommended)
  • 2 tbsp raisins
  • 10 cashews, halved
  • 8 almonds, slivered
  • Pinch of saffron (optional)

Instructions

01

Grate the Carrots

Peel and grate all carrots using the coarse side of a grater. This is the most time-consuming part — but the texture is worth it. Avoid pre-grated carrots; they are too dry.

02

Cook in Milk

Add grated carrots and milk to a wide, heavy-bottomed pan. Bring to a boil, then simmer on medium heat, stirring frequently, for 30–35 minutes until the milk is almost fully absorbed and the mixture thickens.

03

Add Sugar & Cardamom

Add sugar and crushed cardamom. The mixture will loosen as sugar melts — this is normal. Continue cooking and stirring for 10 more minutes until it tightens up again.

04

Fry in Ghee

Push the halwa to one side. Add ghee to the empty space and let it heat. Add the halwa into the ghee and fry, stirring constantly, for 8–10 minutes until it turns a deep reddish-orange and ghee starts to release at the edges.

05

Add Khoya & Dry Fruits

Crumble in the khoya if using — it adds a rich milky depth. Add raisins, cashews, and almonds. Stir and cook 3 more minutes.

06

Serve Warm or Cold

Serve warm, garnished with almond slivers and a pinch of saffron. Also excellent chilled — the flavours deepen overnight. A scoop of vanilla ice cream alongside is not traditional but is deeply right.
